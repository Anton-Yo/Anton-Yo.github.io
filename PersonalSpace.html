Personal Space

In Personal Space, the player must navigate a small procedurally generated galaxy while avoiding spaceships trying to enter your atmosphere. A bit of a bizarre theme, but the theme was Re-Entry, so why not play as the planet trying to avoid that very thing? The only way to escape? Blow up the sun to create a black hole.

This game was developed over 5 days in Macquarie University's Re-entry Game Jam. For the second year in a row, my team and I won the People's Choice award. Play on itch.io.

My Contributions:

  • Creative Director & Producer
    • I came up with the subversive take on the theme and brainstormed it with the team. I was then responsible for maintaining the project scope and vision, while managing the workloads of the other team members
  • Procedural Generation
    • Having completed another project on procedural generation, I decided a procedurally generated galaxy would be a good idea. In a Game Jam. Of course, some compromises had to be made, for simplicity and for the game's overall goal of finding the sun
    • In the end, I wrote a generation script to create a 360 x 360 grid and populate it with planets of various sizes, and asteroid fields of varying scale/rotation
    • The most difficult part of this generation was preventing the astral bodies from intercepting each other. I checked for other objects in a scaled circle around the generated spawn point, and re-randomised if they intercepted
    • Designated the player spawn point as one of the outer quadrants near the bounds of the play area
    • The script spawns objects around the sun object, which is always at the center
  • Smaller Tasks
    • Implemented the enemy ship spawn system and player movement
    • Sourced and implemented the audio, using 3D spatialised audio for the spaceship explosions
    • Created particles effects for ship explosion and planet movement
