Life Cycle of a Banana

Hover over the stages to see what happens!!

Offshoots

Banana plant ‘suckers’ must be taken from the original plant. These ‘suckers’ are offshoots from a banana plant creeping half root, half stem system or ‘rhizome’. Which is also capable of sprouting shoots and roots for a new plant.

Planting new banana plants

The new planting location for the offshoots requires certain conditions to be met. The soil needs to be rich in organic material and therefore compost is recommended. Additionally, the offshoots need to be kept moist until new roots can grow. Budding banana plants will also spring up from the root system of a dead or dying banana plant.

Early plant growth

Bananas aren’t trees, but a perennial herb. This means that they do not have a wooden stem but grow only leaves. The stem of the banana plant is called a pseudostem due to being made up of tightly packed leaf stems.

Maturing plant growth

The tightly packed pseudo stem has older leaves at the bottom and when growing, adds new, smaller leaves towards the top. Banana leaves can grow up to 2-4 metres in length. Root systems continue to develop during this time. This process of maturation takes nearly 6 months.

Flower growth and development

Banana plants begin flowering after 6 months, although this timing depends on the growth conditions. Usually, flowers finishing developing after almost 3 months and are reddish-purple in colour and shaped like teardrops. Banana trees grow male and female flowers but pollination is not required for banana growth.

Early banana growth

Female flowers bloom and begin to form banana bunches, removing layers of reddish-purple ‘petals’ as the stem grows and forming green juvenile bunches of banana underneath

Banana maturation

During the next three months, the fruits will mature. A single banana plant can produce more than 200 bananas, with each bunch containing up to 20 bananas. Bananas bunch branches are harvested while still green on the tree and left to ripen indoors or while transported. If the bananas ripen on the tree, they usually will get damaged.

Banana tree dies :(

Surprisingly, the banana tree dies after producing fruit. The pseudostem dies and is cleared away on plantations. The banana plant will be replaced by baby banana plants or offshoots from the root system.
